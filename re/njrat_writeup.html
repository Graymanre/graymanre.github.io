Introduction

In a recent incident response investigation, multiple workstations were infected by an unknown threat actor using njRat. The initial infection vector started with a phishing email using a malicious document which subsequently downloaded the second stage payload, created persistence and attempted to exfiltrate data obtained from its keylogging functionality. In this technical analysis, we will analyze the infection change, the keylogger and its persistence methods.
